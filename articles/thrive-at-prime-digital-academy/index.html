How to Thrive at Prime Digital Academy

10 Tips for Getting the Most Out of Your Prime Experience

STEVE SAVSTROM / published 27 SEPTEMBER 2021

This year, I made the leap from teaching to tech and completed the full stack software engineering program at Prime Digital Academy. Over the course of 20 weeks, I transformed my software engineering skills from writing my first ‚ÄúHello, World!‚Äù program to building full stack applications with React, Node.js, Express, and PostgreSQL. I learned so much along the way and want to share some tips with future Primers. Here is my advice for getting the most out of your experience:

Build a Community

Success at Prime is largely dependent on the strength of your relationships with others in your cohort. You are not on this journey alone and you will be far more successful if you connect and collaborate with your classmates as much as possible.

üí° Tip: Challenge yourself to meet everyone in your cohort during Tier 1. Reach out on Slack or meet up on Zoom. Build connections, learn about everyone's background and their career aspirations. You will be working closely with each of them over the next few months.

Immerse Yourself

Prime is more than a full time job for 20 weeks (expect 60+ hour weeks.) Design your lifestyle so that Prime can be your top priority. Communicate with your partner, family, and friends about the commitment and talk about creating a healthy schedule so that you can focus on your code and also fulfill other responsibilities.

üí° Tip: Share your calendar and schedule with important people in your life so that you are all on the same page. Mark important dates such as your solo and group project presentations.

Be Present

Prime moves fast and every moment counts. Each piece of the curriculum is created to give you the foundational skills you need to be a successful software engineer. Show up to every lecture, career session, industry speaker, mentor meeting, and study group. Be a sponge and soak up as much as you can.

üí° Tip: Take breaks between sessions to clear your head and allow yourself to move on to what's next. You may want to keep working on a problem or bug from the previous lecture but try to compartmentalize so that you can be fully present in the next session. Take time at the end of the day to circle back and reflect on questions that need more attention.

Be Your Authentic Self

Every Prime student is making a career change. Remember that you are an asset to your cohort and the Prime community. Bring your unique background and experience to your cohort. Offer your perspective, strengths, and experience throughout the program.

üí° Tip: Reflect on the skills you are bringing with you to Prime and find ways to utilize them. Technical, creative, and leadership skills will all be useful throughout Prime as you tackle projects. Having elf-awareness of your unique strengths is also helpful for crafting your resume, cover letters, and your personal narrative heading into your job search. Share Your Code

Don't be afraid to show what you're working on. Make your GitHub repositories public, participate in code reviews with your peers, share your code and ask for help debugging. Live debugging during class can be incredibly helpful to you and your whole cohort.

üí° Tip: Pair programming is a great way to build confidence coding and communicating. Make time to pair program with others in your cohort.

Document Your Process

Make a habit of taking notes, writing descriptive and thorough code comments, posting on LinkedIn, and sharing resources on Slack.

üí° Tip: Writing high-quality code comments is essential for being able to revisit and learn from your previous work. You will be referring to old projects constantly and, without descriptive comments, it can be easy to get lost in your own code.

Be Vulnerable and Ask Questions (Constantly)

Every concept taught at Prime is essential and it is important that you are constantly clarifying your understanding and getting feedback. Questions allow your instructors to reinforce concepts that haven‚Äôt been mastered. If you have a question, others likely do too and it helps everyone if you ask.

üí° Tip: Write your questions down and bring them to class. Be prepared to talk about what you tried already and what didn't work. Have your own code ready to share as an example.

Embrace Collaboration

One of the most important skills you will develop at Prime is the ability to collaborate with diverse teams to build projects. Each week you will be shuffled into new groups and presented with a new problem to solve. Practice navigating this collaborative process and pay attention to how you are growing along the way.

üí° Tip: When starting a new group project, establish team norms and a clear vision for what you want to accomplish. Find consensus for what your goal is and the tools and strategies you will use to achieve it. Document these norms and revisit them throughout the project.

Avoid Comparing Yourself to Others

Remember that everyone's background and experience is different. Establish your own goals and measure your individual progress toward those goals. Your coding journey is not linear and there are many "right" ways to do things. Imposter syndrome will creep in at times but it is a distraction from continuing to invest in your personal growth.

üí° Tip: Revisit your old code and compare your current self to where you were a week ago or a month ago. That's the only comparison that matters. Recognize how far you have come and believe that progress will continue.

Start Your Job Search Early and Build Your Network

It is never too early to polish your resume, draft cover letters, and start applying for jobs. Most Prime students start applying for jobs during Tier 2 but resumes and other materials can be done even earlier. Networking can start immediately and Prime provides a lot of opportunities to connect with alumni through career speakers and mentor groups.

üí° Tip: Listen to Christy! Each week you will have 1-2 hours of career development time with Christy Larsen, Prime's Director of Employer Partnerships. She will provide a roadmap for navigating the application process and getting a job. This includes writing resumes, cover letters, interviewing, networking, applying, and following up on applications. Christy has helped hundreds of new software engineers land their first job, so pay attention and implement her strategies.

Hopefully these tips are helpful as you start your coding journey! Prime was a transformational experience for me as I moved from teaching to tech. These practices helped me succeed and get the most out of the program. You are making a great choice by joining this community and I wish you all the best!
